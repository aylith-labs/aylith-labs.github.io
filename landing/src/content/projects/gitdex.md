## Vision

Once you cross a few dozen repositories, you stop knowing what you own. Gitdex is the index for your estate — it categorizes, scores, and tracks every repo across every org, so questions like "what is active, what is dead, what is duplicated" have answers instead of guesses. It turns a sprawl of repos into a catalog you can actually manage.

## The Problem

Repositories accumulate faster than anyone audits them. Across multiple orgs the picture fragments completely — there is no single view, no scoring, no sense of what is alive. Decisions about what to archive, invest in, or consolidate get made blind because the inventory does not exist.

## Key Differentiators

- **Estate-wide view**: every repo across every org in one place.
- **Scored, not just listed**: health and activity signals, not a raw list.
- **Categorized automatically**: repos sorted by purpose and stack without manual tagging.
- **Tracked over time**: drift and rot become visible instead of accumulating silently.
